About Me

My journey into cybersecurity began with a simple question: how do systems fail when viewed from an attacker's perspective? That curiosity led me from India to Denmark to pursue a Master's degree in Cybersecurity at the Technical University of Denmark. Since then, I have focused on developing an adversarial mindset — not only understanding how systems are built, but how they can be broken, manipulated, and exploited under real-world conditions.

At DTU, I don't just study attacks — I simulate them. I've reverse-engineered Android applications to expose top OWASP vulnerabilities. I've built Python security tools from scratch, run full kill-chain penetration tests with Metasploit, Nmap, JADX, APKTool, FRIDA, ADB and Burp Suite, and conducted cybersecurity risk assessments on critical infrastructure.

My Master's thesis at Philips Medisize pushed this further. I developed HAVA — the Health Apps Vulnerability Assessor — a Python-based automated tool for assessing security requirements in mHealth applications against regulatory standards (FDA, EU MDR, HIPAA, GDPR). I integrated HAVA directly into the GitHub CI/CD pipeline, embedding continuous security assessment into the development lifecycle. The thesis was graded 12 out of 12 — the highest mark possible.

I operate on one conviction: you cannot defend what you don't fully understand. Outside the terminal, I'm a boxer, a dance choreographer, and a volleyball captain. The discipline, pattern recognition, and team coordination those demand? They show up in every security engagement I run.
